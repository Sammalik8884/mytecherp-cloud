2. UpKeep — Best for Maintenance Teams

UpKeep is a popular CMMS (Computerized Maintenance Management System) favored by maintenance-heavy operations. It excels at work order management and preventive maintenance scheduling.

Pros

  • Excellent mobile app for technicians
  • Strong preventive maintenance scheduling
  • Asset management with QR codes

Cons

  • No CRM or invoicing — you'll need separate tools
  • Per-user pricing gets expensive fast ($20-$75/user/mo)
  • Limited quotation and contract management

3. ServiceM8 — Best for Field Service

Built specifically for field service businesses in Australia and beyond. ServiceM8 offers job management, quoting, invoicing, and scheduling in a clean interface.

Pros

  • Excellent scheduling and dispatch features
  • Built-in quoting and invoicing
  • GPS tracking for field staff

Cons

  • No inventory or warehouse management
  • Limited to field service — not ideal for facility management
  • No payroll or HR module

4. Limble CMMS — Best for Maintenance Planning

Limble focuses on maintenance management with strong work order templates, PM scheduling, and asset tracking. Great for manufacturing and property maintenance teams.

Pricing:

From $28/user/month. Per-user pricing means costs scale with team size.

5. Jobber — Best for Home Services

Jobber excels in the home services space — lawn care, cleaning, HVAC. It offers client management, scheduling, and invoicing but lacks depth for commercial FM operations.

Pricing:

From $29/month (Core plan). Doesn't include inventory, assets, or payroll.

6. Loc8 — Best for Asset-Heavy Operations

Loc8 is an Australian-built FM platform focused on asset management and work order tracking. Good for teams that manage equipment-heavy facilities.

7. FieldPulse — Best for Small Contractors

FieldPulse targets small trade contractors (electricians, plumbers, HVAC) with job management, estimates, and basic CRM. Pricing starts at $60/user/mo — expensive for small teams.

8. Hippo CMMS — Best for Building Managers

Hippo is a straightforward building maintenance CMMS with work orders, PM scheduling, and a help desk portal for tenants. Lacks CRM, invoicing, and payroll.

9. Fiix — Best Enterprise CMMS

Owned by Rockwell Automation, Fiix is built for large enterprises with complex maintenance needs. Powerful but complex and expensive — not suitable for SMBs.

10. Odoo — Best Open-Source Option

Odoo is a modular ERP with facility management capabilities through add-ons. While feature-rich, it requires significant customization and technical expertise to set up for FM use cases.

How to Choose the Right FM Software

Ask yourself these questions before deciding:

  1. Do you need just maintenance, or full business management? — If just maintenance, a CMMS like UpKeep or Limble works. If you need CRM + quoting + invoicing + work orders, choose MytechERP or ServiceM8.
  2. How many users will you have? — Per-user pricing (UpKeep, Limble, FieldPulse) gets expensive fast. Per-plan pricing (MytechERP, Jobber) is more predictable.
  3. Do you manage physical sites and buildings? — MytechERP's site hierarchy (Sites → Buildings → Floors → Rooms) is unique and essential for facilities management.
  4. Do you need payroll and HR? — Only MytechERP includes built-in payroll. Others require separate HR software.
  5. What's your budget? — For under $100/month, MytechERP gives you the most complete feature set.
